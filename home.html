Welcome

This is the Central Hub for Monitoring Industrial Environmental Impact.

Air Pollution (PM2.5)

PM2.5 refers to fine particulate matter with a diameter of 2.5 micrometers or less. These particles can penetrate deep into the lungs and bloodstream, causing significant health issues. The national standard for industrial areas is 60 µg/m³ annually.

Water Pollution (BOD)

Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD) measures the amount of oxygen consumed by bacteria while decomposing organic matter in water. High BOD levels indicate significant organic pollution. The standard for discharge into inland surface water is typically below 30 mg/L.

Sound Pollution (dBA)

Sound pollution is measured in decibels (dBA). In industrial zones, high noise levels are generated by machinery and transportation, which can cause health problems. The permissible limit for industrial areas in India is 75 dBA during the daytime.

Land Pollution (Waste)

This metric tracks the generation of hazardous waste. Improper disposal can contaminate soil and groundwater, posing long-term environmental risks. Minimizing waste generation is a key goal for sustainable industrial practices.

Pollution Level Thresholds

Pollution Type Low (Blue) Moderate (Orange) High (Red)
Air Pollution (PM2.5) Below 50 µg/m³ 50 - 99 µg/m³ 100 µg/m³ & above
Water Pollution (BOD) Below 40 mg/L 40 - 79 mg/L 80 mg/L & above
Sound Pollution (dBA) Below 65 dBA 65 - 84 dBA 85 dBA & above
Land Pollution (Waste) Below 20 tons 20 - 49 tons 50 tons & above
